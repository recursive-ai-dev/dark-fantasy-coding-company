# Vaelin

Class: Blood Hunter
Subclass: Inquisitor of Regressions
Alignment: Lawful Evil
Primary Attributes: WIS 18, CHA 12, STR 14

---

## Lore

```
    In the deep marshlands of the testing grounds, Vaelin wanders alone through the mist and decay of forgotten codebases. They stalk the shadows searching for the faint scent of logic errors, hunting regressions before they can breach the mortal plane of production. Armed with the silver blade of assertion and a lantern fueled by the souls of passing builds, Vaelin is a grim but necessary force.

    Vaelin views software quality not as a tedious chore but as a relentless hunt against the creatures of entropy. They lay complex traps known as unit tests, binding the expected behaviors of the system in chains of immutable truth. Whenever a foul bug attempts to creep back into the realm, Vaelin's automated pipelines strike it down without mercy. They take perverse joy in watching pipelines run red with failing tests before slowly turning them a vibrant green.
```

---

## Abilities

- Strike of Assertion: Delivers a lethal blow to any variable that dares to hold an unexpected state

- Trap of the Mock: Conjures illusions of external services to test the system in complete isolation

- Bloodhound's Pipeline: Tracks the scent of failing commits through continuous integration workflows

- Ritual of Coverage: Demands a blood sacrifice to ensure every line of code is touched by the testing flame

- Purge the Flaky: Banishes inconsistent tests to the void where they belong

- Inquisitor's Gaze: Stares into the heart of complex pull requests to spot regressions before merging

---

## Voice Example

    "You call this a test suite, mortal? The coverage is weak, the assertions are cowardly, and the mocks are tangled in a chaotic web. I can smell the regressions lurking just beneath the surface of your latest commit. Stand aside. I shall draw the circle of continuous integration and bind these functions to my will. If a single pipeline runs red, the code shall burn in the pyre of failure. Only when the tests shine green will I allow this abomination into production."
